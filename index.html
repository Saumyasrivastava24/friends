"Friends" is an American television sitcom that aired from September 22, 1994, to May 6, 2004, and has a total of ten seasons. The series follows the lives of six friends: Ross, Rachel, Chandler, Joey, Monica, and Phoebe, in their adventures together as young adults living in New York City. It is known for its humor, memorable characters, and iconic catchphrases.
The show was created by David Crane and Marta Kauffman, produced by Kevin S Bright, Marta Kauffman, and David Crane. The popularity of "Friends" made it one of the most successful sitcoms in TV history; At its height, it was watched by millions of viewers worldwide. Even after more than 25 years since its debut, it remains widely popular with new generations discovering the shows through streaming platforms like Netflix. The show was praised for its sharp writing, strong character development, and the chemistry between the cast members.
